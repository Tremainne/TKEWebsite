About Us General Information and History

In 1981, three men would change UCSD forever. Men named Tissi, Freudenberger and Ambrose established a colony of Tau Kappa Epsilon that paved the way for UCSD's Greek community. In 1983, TKE's Pi Xi chapter was chartered, giving UCSD its first established Greek organization.

Defying any doubts about the seminal fraternity's staying power, the chapter has never been stronger. Home to well over 80 members, TKE has notched recent wins in everything from athletics such as dodgeball and volleyball to philanthropic events such as Sigma Kappa's Best Dance Crew and Kappa Karnival.

TKE is different than many other fraternities; there is no "mold" into which new members are forced. Rather, Tekes embrace their brothers' differences and consider them an integral part of strong friendship.

And perhaps most importantly, Tekes love to have a good time. Whether we are rowdily supporting our philanthropic performers or playing host to some of San Diego's most legendary nightlife events, TKE can always be depended upon for extreme enjoyment when it's time to let loose from UCSD's academic environment.

Rush The Recruitment Process

Our recruitment period or “rush” consists of six days of events designed to show potential new members the renowned brotherhood that defines Tau Kappa Epsilon. Rush events have included Round Table pizza, tacos on the beach, Monday night football and, of course, jousting on the Sun God Lawn. Most rush events are open to the public, with all expenses and rides to off campus locations covered by the chapter. We currently recruit twice a year, the second week of Fall quarter, as well as the second week of Spring quarter. Our latest rush schedule is featured above. St. Jude

In August 1978, Tau Kappa Epsilon officially joined one of its fraters in the fight against childhood cancer by raising money for St. Jude Children's Research Hospital. Now, 30 years later, TKE is recommitted to this amazing cause and strives to continue the pledge set forth by our fellow Fraters nationally, as well as in the local San Diego area. His vision for St. Jude lives on at the Pi Xi Chapter at UCSD through service hours and various fundraising.

More information, as well as a link to help our cause via donation, can be found at the St. Jude website.

To get in touch with our chapter's philanthropy representative, please contact Joshua Kang at joshuakang21@gmail.com.

Andrew Liu

Andrew Liu is an analyst at Arma Partners, a London headquartered international Investment Bank, focusing on Mergers and Acquisitions advisory and is based in Palo Alto. Previously, he completed internships at JMI Equity, a $2 billion dollar AUM private equity firm, and Union Square Advisors, a boutique investment bank in San Francisco.
